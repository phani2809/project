Sambar Idly

Ingredients

  • 6-8 idlis (prepared using the plain idly recipe)
  • 1 cup toor dal (pigeon peas)
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 1 tomato, chopped
  • 1 carrot, chopped
  • 1 potato, chopped
  • 1/2 cup pumpkin, chopped
  • 2 tbsp sambar powder
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1/4 tsp asafoetida (hing)
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tbsp tamarind pulp
  • 2 tbsp oil
  • 1 tsp mustard seeds
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 2 dried red chilies
  • Few curry leaves
  • Fresh coriander leaves, for garnish

Recipe

Step 1: Rinse the toor dal and pressure cook it with 2 cups of water until soft and mushy. Mash the cooked dal and set aside.

Step 2: Heat oil in a large pan. Add mustard seeds, cumin seeds, dried red chilies, and curry leaves. Sauté until the seeds splutter.

Step 3: Add chopped onions and sauté until they turn translucent. Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they become soft.

Step 4: Add chopped carrot, potato, and pumpkin. Sauté for a few minutes. Add turmeric powder, asafoetida, sambar powder, and salt. Mix well.

Step 5: Add 3 cups of water and bring it to a boil. Reduce the heat and simmer until the vegetables are cooked.

Step 6: Add the mashed dal to the pan and mix well. Add tamarind pulp and simmer for another 10-15 minutes, adjusting the consistency with water if needed.

Step 7: Arrange the idlis in serving bowls and pour hot sambar over them. Let the idlis soak in the sambar for a few minutes.

Step 8: Garnish with fresh coriander leaves and serve hot.

Time Required

Preparation Time: 30 minutes

Cooking Time: 30 minutes
