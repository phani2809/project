Plain Idly

Ingredients

  • 2 cups idly rice
  • 1 cup urad dal (split black gram)
  • 1/2 tsp fenugreek seeds
  • Salt to taste
  • Water, as needed
  • Oil, for greasing

Recipe

Step 1: Rinse the idly rice and urad dal separately until the water runs clear. Soak the rice and dal along with fenugreek seeds in water for at least 6-8 hours or overnight.

Step 2: Drain the soaked urad dal and fenugreek seeds. Grind them in a blender or wet grinder, adding water gradually, to make a smooth and fluffy batter.

Step 3: Drain the soaked rice and grind it to a slightly coarse batter, adding water as needed.

Step 4: Combine both batters in a large bowl, add salt, and mix well. The batter should be of dropping consistency. Cover the bowl and let it ferment in a warm place for 8-12 hours or until it doubles in volume.

Step 5: Once fermented, gently stir the batter. Grease the idly molds with oil and pour the batter into each mold, filling them about 3/4 full.

Step 6: Steam the idlis in an idly steamer or pressure cooker (without the whistle) for about 10-12 minutes on medium heat, or until a toothpick inserted in the center comes out clean.

Step 7: Remove the idly molds from the steamer and let them cool for a few minutes. Use a spoon to gently remove the idlis from the molds.

Step 8: Serve hot with chutney and sambar.

Time Required

Preparation Time: 20 minutes (excluding soaking and fermentation time)

Cooking Time: 15 minutes
